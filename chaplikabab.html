Chapli Kabab

Chapli Kabab is a famous dish of Peshawar

Ingredients

  • 500g ground meat
  • 1 finely chopped onion
  • 2-3 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • 2-3 tablespoons chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 2-3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2-3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 egg (optional, for binding)
  • 2-3 tablespoons besan (gram flour, optional, for binding)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the ground meat, chopped onion, green chilies, coriander powder, cumin seeds, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, chopped coriander leaves, chopped mint leaves, and minced garlic.
  2. Mix everything together thoroughly.
  3. If the mixture feels too loose, you can add an egg or besan (gram flour) to bind it. This step is optional.
  4. Heat cooking oil in a pan.
  5. Take a portion of the mixture and shape it into a flat, round patty (kabab).
  6. Carefully place the kabab in the hot oil and cook until it's browned and cooked through on both sides. You can also grill or bake the kababs if you prefer a healthier option.
  7. Once cooked, remove the chapli kababs from the pan and place them on paper towels to drain any excess oil.
  8. Serve hot with naan, roti, or as desired, along with yogurt, chutney, and salad.
